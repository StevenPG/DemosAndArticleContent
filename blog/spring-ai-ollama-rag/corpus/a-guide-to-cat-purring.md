# A Guide to Cat Purring

Purring is one of the most recognizable sounds a cat makes, but it doesn't
always mean what people assume.

## How purring works

Cats purr by rapidly contracting the muscles of their larynx, which
modulates airflow during both inhalation and exhalation. This produces the
low, steady vibration most people associate with a content cat.

## It's not only about happiness

While cats often purr when relaxed or being petted, they also purr when
anxious, injured, or even while giving birth. Some researchers believe the
frequency range of a purr (around 25-150 Hz) may promote healing in bones and
tissue, which could explain why cats purr in stressful situations too.

## Kittens and purring

Kittens can purr within the first few days of life, long before they can see
or hear well. It's thought to be a way of communicating contentment to their
mother during nursing.
