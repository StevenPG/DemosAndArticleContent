# Why Cats Knock Things Off Tables

Every cat owner has watched their cat stare directly at a glass, place a paw on
it, and push it off the edge of a table without breaking eye contact. This
isn't random mischief — it's a mix of instinct and curiosity.

## Testing the environment

Cats are natural hunters, and part of hunting is understanding how objects in
their territory behave. Batting an item off a ledge lets a cat learn whether
it moves, rolls, or reacts — useful information for an animal wired to track
small prey.

## Attention seeking

If knocking a pen off the desk reliably gets you to look up from your laptop,
your cat has learned a very effective button to press. Cats are quick to
repeat behaviors that produce a reaction, even a mildly annoyed one.

## It might just be fun

Paws are a cat's primary tool for interacting with the world. Swatting things
exercises that instinct in a low-stakes way, especially for indoor cats who
don't get to chase much else during the day.
